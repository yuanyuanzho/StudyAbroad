5 UK universities you should consider for your Computing degree

Johnson | April 6, 2017

The digital age has spurred a demand for professionals in the fields of computing and computer science. Based on the findings of recent reports, the demand isn’t slowing down any time soon, which is great for prospective students who are keen to forge a career in this respected discipline.

According to a report from The Complete University Guide, the UK job market is experiencing a shortage of graduates with digital expertise. “In the software industry alone there are 20,000 graduate vacancies a year, and only 7,500 computer science graduates to fill them,” says Hugh Millward, Microsoft’s Director of Corporate Affairs.

Meanwhile, a report by Computer World UK has shown that the most popular jobs of 2017 includes the IT profession, with research by the global IT industry body CompTIA reporting that UK IT staff job postings have increased by 10 percent each year. On top of that, the emerging threats of cyber hacking and identity theft have led to a boost in corporate security measures, with companies worldwide seeking to hire professionals who specialise in cyber security to protect their business assets.

If this field is truly of interest to you, a degree in Computing is exactly what you need. A general degree covers many areas, giving students the chance to explore subject areas before selecting their specialisation. Among the most common higher education pathways are disciplines like Computer Science, Cyber Security, Information Technology and Software Engineering. Courses are known to hold a practical-focus, equipping students with subject-specific and transferable skills that make them highly employable graduates.

Here are 5 UK universities you should consider to kick-start your career in computing…

SCHOOL OF PHYSICAL SCIENCES AND COMPUTING, UNIVERSITY OF CENTRAL LANCASHIRE (UCLan)

This School provides an innovative computing degree that promises to equip graduates with the technical skills the industry demands, on top of the entrepreneurial and social expertise that grant a competitive edge. From Computer Games Development, to Computing Network Technology, to Forensic Computing and beyond, UCLan offers a breadth and depth of courses – all of which provide invaluable optional placement years – to produce creative students with the proficiencies to work with rapidly changing technologies.

SCHOOL OF COMPUTING, UNIVERSITY OF KENT

At Kent’s School of Computing, Faculty aim to do more than provide students with a first-rate academic experience. Here, you’ll develop key transferable skills that are considered vital for a successful career. The staff ensure that pupils pursuing a Computing degree are equipped with the knowledge and proficiencies that will make them highly sought-after in the graduate sphere.

DEPARTMENT OF COMPUTING AND COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGIES, OXFORD BROOKES UNIVERSITY

Oxford Brookes University promises a lively, progressive and supportive study environment. At the Department of Computing and Communication Technologies, the Faculty believes in producing well-rounded graduates that develop a wide range of cognitive, practical, analytical, creative and professional expertise.

FACULTY OF COMPUTING AND ENGINEERING, ULSTER UNIVERSITY

With over 92 percent of graduates in work or further study six months after graduation, it’s clear that a degree from Ulster University can strengthen your future employment opportunities. The Faculty of Computing and Engineering offers industry-led and professionally recognised degrees at both undergraduate and postgraduate level. The programmes include a mix of theory and application with a focus on employability. Each teaching course is underpinned by world-class research and established industry links.

SCHOOL OF ENGINEERING AND INFORMATICS, UNIVERSITY OF SUSSEX

With an excellent record for teaching and research in the Computing field, there’s no better place than Sussex to kick-start your education. The School of Engineering and Informatics offers a rich and stimulating curriculum that connects computer science with a broad range of application areas. Many of its degrees are interdisciplinary, drawing on and contributing to subjects like business and management, media studies and more.
